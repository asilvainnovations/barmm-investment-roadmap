Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common investment and facilitation questions from prospective investors and partners.

BARMM's Strategic Investment Priorities Plan (SIPP) identifies 13 priority categories. Key sectors include halal food & beverage, halal cosmetics and pharmaceuticals, Islamic finance (banking, Takaful, Sukuk), agribusiness and fisheries, renewable energy, tourism and hospitality, digital infrastructure, and ecozone-based manufacturing. The Bangsamoro Board of Investments (BBOI) can provide sector-specific incentive packages.

The Bangsamoro Halal Board (BHB) is the regional certification authority. Certification processes are being aligned with OIC/SMIIC international standards covering food (OIC/SMIIC 1:2019), cosmetics (OIC/SMIIC 4:2018), tourism (OIC/SMIIC 9:2019), and supply chain management (OIC/SMIIC 17:2020). Contact our investment facilitation team at invest@mtit.bangsamoro.gov.ph to initiate the BHB referral process.

The BBOI and Bangsamoro Economic Zone Authority (BEZA) offer a range of fiscal incentives aligned with the national Strategic Investment Priorities Plan (SIPP) and the Bangsamoro Organic Law's autonomous fiscal provisions. These include income tax holidays, tax and duty exemptions on capital equipment imports, and special ecozone privileges at PFEZ and WOW Matanog SEZ. Contact BBOI directly through our facilitation desk for a customised incentive package based on your investment profile.

BARMM is targeting a 1-day business registration time through the Bangsamoro Business Name Registration (BBNR) digital platform under the Bangsamoro e-Governance Master Plan (BEGMP). The one-stop investment facilitation centres are designed to process permits and licences within 7 days once the system reaches full operationalisation (targeted by 2028). Current processing times vary by municipality; our desk can advise on the fastest pathway for your specific business type.

BARMM has made significant strides since the signing of the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB) in 2014 and the enactment of the Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL) in 2018. Poverty incidence has fallen from 55.9% in 2018 to 23.5% in 2023, and new investments reached ₱3.5 billion in 2024. Mainland provinces such as Maguindanao del Norte, Maguindanao del Sur, Lanao del Sur, Basilan, and Cotabato City have active and growing business communities. Our facilitation team can provide current security advisories and connect you with existing investors for ground-level perspectives.

The Bangsamoro Islamic Finance Roadmap (2024–2028) targets a functional Shariah-compliant financial system by 2028. Development priorities include Islamic banking foundations, microfinance and Waqf (Islamic endowment) instruments, Takaful (Islamic insurance) operations, and a Sukuk (Islamic bond) capital market. The roadmap aligns with national Islamic banking legislation and regional development goals. Contact our team for referrals to Islamic banking partners currently operating in the region.
