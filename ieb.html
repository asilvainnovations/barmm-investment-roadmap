Reimagining BARMM’s Economic Sectors: Building an Integrated Investment Roadmap (2026–2036)

By Alvin M. Silva

Updated: April 2026 • Regional Development Strategy

Executive Overview

This paper outlines a systemic transformation of the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) economy from a fragmented sectoral approach to an integrated ecosystem model. By aligning foundational industries with transformative value creation and robust enablers, BARMM can achieve sustainable growth anchored in moral governance.

  • Growth: Economy grew 2.7% in 2024 despite structural challenges.
  • Momentum: Registered ₱5 billion in investments in 2025 with strong focus on Halal and Agriculture.
  • Strategy: Adoption of Systems Thinking to break silos between Agriculture, Industry, Finance, and Infrastructure.
  • Governance: Moral Governance positioned as the operating system for all economic activities.

Understanding BARMM’s Current Economic Landscape

Growth Trajectory

BARMM’s economy grew by 2.7% in 2024. While modest compared to national averages, this reflects progress amidst historical fragility and underinvestment.

Source: [1]

Sector Composition

  • Services: 42%
  • Agriculture/Forestry/Fisheries: 32.4%
  • Industry: 25.6%
Source: [1]

The contraction of agriculture by 4.2% and mining by 8.7% highlights vulnerabilities in resource-based sectors, signaling that current siloed operations are insufficient for long-term stability.

Investment Momentum

Despite structural constraints, investment momentum is strong. In 2025, BARMM achieved ₱5 billion in total registered investments, generating over 2,000 jobs, surpassing its annual targets within the first quarter via the Board of Investments ([[2]] & [[3]]).

Critically, approvals concentrated in high-impact areas like healthcare, infrastructure, and the halal industry reflect a strategic pivot toward comparative advantages. The approval of a ₱133.91 billion Annual Investment Program for 2026 underscores institutional commitment to development ([[5]]).

Why Reorganization of Economic Sectors Matters

Traditional planning treats sectors as isolated units (Agriculture vs. Industry vs. Finance). However, operational bottlenecks exist when agricultural output outpaces logistics or when tourism lacks supporting infrastructure ([[Systemic Analysis]]).

Systems thinking is required to shift focus from isolated outputs to flows, relationships, and feedback loops.

Moral Governance as the Core Operating System

Embedded Infrastructure

Moral governance is not a separate component but the operating system underlying all sectors. It emphasizes transparency, inclusivity, sustainability, and peace-building.

Without it, economic strategies fail due to lack of legitimacy. With it, governance acts as the functional foundation (roots) nourishing the entire tree of economic activity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is BARMM reorganizing its economic sectors?

To move from fragmented planning to an integrated system that improves efficiency, investment impact, and long-term growth.

What makes BARMM's framework unique?

The integration of moral governance as the core operating system guiding all economic activities, ensuring social cohesion and environmental integrity.

How does Islamic finance support the economy?

It provides ethical, inclusive funding aligned with local values, enabling investments across sectors where conventional banking may face cultural resistance.
