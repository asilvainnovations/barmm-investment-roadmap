The Blueprint

The BEIE Framework

The Bangsamoro Economic and Investment Ecosystem (BEIE) Framework is the structural blueprint for the Investment Roadmap 2026–2035. Every sector, policy, and initiative maps onto this integrated model — with Moral Governance as its ethical anchor.

No single layer thrives in isolation: agriculture needs logistics, industry needs energy, exports need standards. All require the trust that only ethical governance can sustain.

🌾
Foundations
Agriculture, fisheries, forestry, and energy — the raw material base for all production.
🏭
Transformers
Halal manufacturing and industry transforming raw resources into high-value global products.
🔧
Enablers
Infrastructure, logistics, health, and education — structural systems that support the workforce.
🌏
Connectors
Export channels, BIMP-EAGA integration, and tourism bridging BARMM to international markets.
💰
Financiers
Islamic banking, Takaful, and Sukuk providing Shariah-compliant capital flows to empower all layers.

The Bangsamoro Economic and Investment Ecosystem

The full ecosystem diagram reveals how every economic actor — from fisherfolk to fintech — integrates within a single ethical framework. Moral Governance is not a layer; it is the central anchor aligning values with action across all sectors.

Strategic Framework

12-Point Priority Agenda

The 2nd Bangsamoro Development Plan (2023–2028) targets 8–9% annual growth driven by a shift from post-conflict fragility to structured, systems-grounded governance. These twelve priorities map directly onto the BEIE Framework's leverage points.

01 · FOUNDATION

⚡ Energy Security

Sustainable energy master plan targeting 80–100% electrification by 2028–2030. Directly removes the binding growth constraint identified in the B1 Limits to Growth archetype.

02 · FOUNDATION

🌾 Agri-Fishery Productivity

Food security through modernised farming and fisheries — forming the raw-material backbone of halal value chains and the BEIE Foundations layer.

03 · ENABLER

🏗️ Strategic Infrastructure

Transportation, communication, and connectivity networks raising the economic growth ceiling. Leverage Point LP2 in the Systems Thinking analysis.

04 · GOVERNANCE

💰 Revenue Generation

Fiscal autonomy and resource mobilisation to reduce block grant dependency to 70% or below — funding the LP3 governance reforms and institutional strengthening.

05 · ENABLER

🌐 Digital Infrastructure

BEGMP 2024–2033 targeting 70% internet penetration and 20 e-governance platforms by 2030, enabling 1-day business registration (LP3 activation).

06 · GOVERNANCE

🏛️ Stronger Bureaucracy

Institutional capacity building directly activating the R2 Governance–Investor Confidence reinforcing loop — the system's most powerful positive feedback mechanism.

07 · FOUNDATION

🕌 Culture & Heritage

Preservation of Bangsamoro identity reinforcing the halal legitimacy that forms BARMM's non-replicable competitive advantage in the global Islamic economy.

08 · CRITICAL

☮️ Peace & Security

Justice system enhancement and normalization completion — essential to dismantling the B2 Security–Investment Tensions loop that can actively reverse development momentum.

09 · FOUNDATION

🤝 Moral Rehabilitation

Community reconciliation grounding investment promotion in Islamic ethical principles — embodying the BEIE Framework's central Moral Governance anchor.

10 · ENABLER

📚 Quality Education

Holistic education aligned with industry demand, addressing the Limits to Growth archetype — the skills shortage that imposes the most persistent growth ceiling on BARMM.

11 · RESILIENCE

🌊 Climate Adaptation

Disaster resilience protecting the agriculture-dependent halal value chain base from climate vulnerabilities — typhoons, flooding, and resource stress across the region.

12 · ENABLER

🏥 Universal Healthcare

Social protection and Ibadah-friendly healthcare integrating Islamic principles — sustaining the workforce capacity that powers BARMM's economic transformation at scale.

Systems Thinking

4 Critical Leverage Points

In systems thinking, leverage points are places in a complex system where a small, well-placed intervention creates disproportionate positive change. These four structural levers shift the entire investment climate.

LP1
Leverage Point 1

Halal Certification System Integrity

BHB achieving OIC/SMIIC international recognition is the single most impactful lever for unlocking access to the USD 2T+ global halal market. Without accreditation, BARMM products cannot enter OIC member markets — 57 countries and 1.8 billion consumers.

→ Target: BHB OIC/SMIIC accreditation by 2029–2032 (Phase 2)
LP2
Leverage Point 2

Infrastructure-Energy-Connectivity Nexus

With only 39% electrification today, energy and digital connectivity constraints impose a hard ceiling on economic growth — the B1 Limits to Growth balancing loop. Investing in the constraint before growth plateaus is the only viable strategy.

→ Target: 80% electrification 2028 · 100% by 2030 · 70% internet 2030
LP3
Leverage Point 3

Governance–Investor Confidence Feedback

Systemic governance reforms — 1-day digital BNR, transparent BHB accreditation, BBOI/BEZA one-stop facilitation — activate the R2 reinforcing loop. This loop runs in reverse when institutions weaken, making governance the riskiest and highest-reward lever.

→ Target: 1-day business registration · Fully digital investment facilitation
LP4
Leverage Point 4

Islamic Finance Ecosystem Development

Shariah-compliant capital is the engine of sustainable halal industry growth. A functional Islamic finance ecosystem by 2028 — banking, Takaful, Sukuk — unlocks OIC capital markets and positions BARMM as ASEAN's emerging Shariah-finance gateway.

→ Target: Full Islamic finance system 2028 · Sukuk operations 2027–2028
